About the site

My name is William Weatherholtz, and I'm a mechanical engineer with a background in robotics, programming, and electronics. I'm passionate about automation and creative problem solving.

On this blog, I'll discuss a variety of technical issues and tools, starting with a well defined engineering problem. By focusing on the technical problem at hand, I hope to future-proof lessons learned, so that better solutions can be made more obvious. I'll try not to shoehorn you into a solution just because it's easy.

This blog grew out of the relative lack of good physical robotics insight available online, and the inclination of engineering-y blogs that do exist to focus on flashy hack-job solutions to problems. This sensationalistic focus leaves many readers without an understanding of things past the trivial level. While I'll discuss fundamentals on this blog, my aim is to provide a more excellent intermediate level understanding here.

This blog also serves as a repository for my projects, which are always under the Projects Category. Notable projects are also listed in the sidebar on this page.
