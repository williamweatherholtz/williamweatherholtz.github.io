About the site

My name is William Weatherholtz, and I'm a mechanical engineer with a background in robotics, programming, and electronics. I'm passionate about leveraging automation for everyday life, and creative problem solving.

On this blog, I'll discuss a variety of technical tools, starting from the top with a well-defined problem and working down to a solution. By using this top-down method, I hope to future-proof lessons learned, both so that better technologies can be recognized as better solutions and that solutions can be applied to similar problems.

With most posts, I'll provide practial questions the reader can ask themselves to gauge their understanding. By providing questions, I hope to help myself more fully understand these subjects by exploring them more deeply, and to help the reader by giving a way to challenge and check themselves. I won't provide answers along with questions, since I believe learning is mostly a function of effort, but I'm willing to discuss anything in the comments including specific answers.

This blog also serves as a repository for my projects, which are always under the Projects Category. Notable projects are also listed in the sidebar on this page.
